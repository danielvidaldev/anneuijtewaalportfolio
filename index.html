About

My name is Anne Uijttewaal and I’m an illustrator and photographer, living and working in Utrecht, The Netherlands. Graduating from the Academy of Arts with a bachelor in Illustration Design and a master in Art Education, I have always focused on how to narrate a story.

I make art so I won’t lose my own creativity and I intend to create the same effect for others. Creativity is closely connected to imagination and intuition, something I feel like the importance of it is easily forgotten.

My work is characterized by its simplicity, openness and somewhat minimalistic approach. In this way it will be easier for viewers to free their own imagination. By leaving out as much elements as possible in my illustrations, my aim is to tell as little as possible, so the part not being told is free to fulfil by the viewer.

As a photographer, openness and simplicity are also important characteristics in my work. Working analogue forces me to think and slow down. I put a focus on the bigger picture, overwhelmed by the diverseness of nature, and narrate what I see through the lens by looking at colour and composition. To me, both photography and illustration share those characteristics, but keep my work diverse at the same time.
